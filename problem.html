The Problem We Address

Understanding the challenges facing teenage girls

What we Address

Teenage girls in marginalized communities face unique and complex challenges that can significantly impact their education, health, and future opportunities. Understanding these challenges is essential for developing effective interventions.

The Context

In many communities where we work, entrenched gender inequalities, economic hardships, and limited access to resources create significant barriers for teenage girls. These challenges are often compounded by:

  • Traditional gender norms that limit girls' opportunities
  • Poverty and economic pressures on families
  • Limited educational and health infrastructure
  • Lack of role models and support networks
  • Early exposure to gender-based violence and exploitation

These factors contribute to alarming statistics: high rates of school dropout, early marriage, teenage pregnancy, and limited economic prospects for young women.

Key Challenges

Educational Barriers

  • School fees and related costs
  • Long distances to schools
  • Lack of sanitary products causing absenteeism
  • Gender-based discrimination in education
  • Pressure to prioritize domestic responsibilities

Health Challenges

  • Limited access to reproductive health information
  • Inadequate health services for adolescents
  • High rates of sexually transmitted infections
  • Mental health issues and limited support
  • Poor nutrition and food insecurity

Early Marriage & Pregnancy

  • Cultural practices supporting child marriage
  • Economic incentives for families (dowry)
  • High rates of teenage pregnancy
  • Complications from early childbearing
  • Limited options after pregnancy or marriage

Violence & Exploitation

  • Gender-based violence in schools and communities
  • Sexual exploitation and abuse
  • Human trafficking
  • Limited legal protections and enforcement
  • Social stigma preventing reporting

Economic Challenges

  • Limited employment opportunities
  • Lack of vocational training
  • Financial dependence on others
  • Exploitation in informal work settings
  • Limited access to financial services

Voice & Agency

  • Limited participation in decision-making
  • Lack of confidence and self-efficacy
  • Restricted mobility and social networks
  • Limited role models and mentors
  • Social norms discouraging leadership

Cycle of Disadvantage

  1. Limited Education

    Girls drop out of school, losing critical opportunities for knowledge, growth, and independence.

  2. Health Issues

    Teen mothers face serious health risks due to inadequate care and unsafe pregnancies.

  3. Poverty

    Teen mothers often remain trapped in poverty with little access to support or resources.

  4. Early Marriage

    Out-of-school girls are often forced into early marriage, ending their childhood and dreams.

  5. Early Pregnacy

    Unplanned teenage pregnancies disrupt education and expose girls to lifelong challenges.

  6. Limited Economic Options

    Lack of skills and education leaves teen mothers with few viable paths to earn a living.

Many of these challenges are interconnected, creating a cycle of disadvantage that can be difficult to break. For example, a girl who drops out of school due to costs or family pressures may be more likely to marry early, which often leads to early pregnancy, health complications, limited economic opportunities, and continued poverty.

Breaking this cycle requires comprehensive interventions that address multiple aspects of a girl's life and the broader social and economic context in which she lives.
