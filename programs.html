Programs & Initiatives

Our strategies for creating meaningful impact

Our programs and initiatives translate our mission into action, addressing the specific needs of teenage girls through evidence-based interventions. Each program is designed to create measurable impact while adapting to local contexts.

Our Flagship Programs

Education & Empowerment Ages 13-18

Girls Leadership Academy

Our signature leadership development program that equips teenage girls with essential life skills, confidence, and knowledge to become agents of change in their communities.

Key Components:

  • Weekly after-school sessions for one academic year
  • Curriculum covering leadership, communication, critical thinking, and advocacy
  • Mentorship from successful women in various fields
  • Community action projects designed and implemented by participants
  • Annual leadership summit bringing together girls from different communities

Impact Highlights:

  • 2,500+ girls trained since program inception
  • 98% of participants report increased confidence
  • 85% demonstrate improved decision-making skills
  • 200+ community projects implemented by participants
Education Ages 12-19

Stay in School Initiative

A comprehensive program aimed at reducing school dropout rates among teenage girls by addressing the multiple barriers they face in accessing and completing education.

Key Components:

  • Scholarships covering school fees, uniforms, and supplies
  • Provision of sanitary products to reduce menstruation-related absenteeism
  • Tutoring and academic support to improve performance
  • Parent engagement workshops to strengthen family support for girls' education
  • Advocacy for girl-friendly school policies and environments

Impact Highlights:

  • 1,800 scholarships provided to date
  • 85% reduction in dropout rates among program participants
  • 70% improvement in academic performance
  • 40,000+ sanitary pads distributed
Health & Wellbeing Ages 12-19

Healthy Futures

A comprehensive health program providing teenage girls with knowledge, skills, and resources to make informed decisions about their health and wellbeing.

Key Components:

  • Age-appropriate sexual and reproductive health education
  • Mental health awareness and support services
  • Peer education training on health topics
  • Mobile health clinics in underserved communities
  • Referral system connecting girls to youth-friendly health services

Impact Highlights:

  • 3,500 girls reached with health education
  • 300 peer educators trained
  • 65% reduction in teen pregnancy in program communities
  • 2,200 girls connected to health services
Economic Empowerment Ages 16-19

Future Entrepreneurs

An economic empowerment program that equips older teenage girls with financial literacy, entrepreneurship skills, and resources to build sustainable livelihoods.

Key Components:

  • Financial literacy training covering savings, budgeting, and basic accounting
  • Entrepreneurship skills development
  • Business plan development and mentoring
  • Seed grants for selected business ideas
  • Savings groups and peer support networks

Impact Highlights:

  • 1,200 girls trained in financial literacy
  • 500+ savings groups established
  • 150 small businesses launched with seed funding
  • 75% of participants report increased income
Community Engagement All ages

Community Champions

A program that engages parents, teachers, community leaders, and other stakeholders to create supportive environments for girls' empowerment and development.

Key Components:

  • Parent education workshops on supporting girls' education and development
  • Teacher training on gender-responsive pedagogy
  • Community dialogues on harmful practices and gender norms
  • Male engagement initiatives targeting fathers, brothers, and male leaders
  • Community action planning for girls' rights and opportunities

Impact Highlights:

  • 5,000+ parents reached through workshops
  • 350 teachers trained in gender-responsive teaching
  • 25 communities developed action plans for girls' rights
  • 60% reduction in reported cases of gender-based violence
Digital Inclusion Ages 14-19

Digital Girls

A program that bridges the digital gender gap by providing teenage girls with access to technology, digital literacy skills, and opportunities in STEM fields.

Key Components:

  • Computer literacy training and digital skills development
  • Access to technology through community computer centers
  • Coding and web development workshops
  • STEM career exposure and mentorship
  • Online safety and digital citizenship education

Impact Highlights:

  • 1,500 girls trained in digital literacy
  • 5 community tech centers established
  • 300 girls completed coding bootcamps
  • 45 girls pursuing STEM higher education

Special Initiatives

Girls Voices Campaign

An annual advocacy campaign that amplifies the voices of teenage girls on issues that affect them, reaching policymakers and the public through various media channels.

Activities include: Youth-led media productions, public exhibitions, social media campaigns, policy dialogues, and community forums.

Annual Girls' Summit

A flagship event bringing together program participants, partners, community members, and thought leaders to celebrate achievements, share learnings, and strengthen the movement for girls' rights.

Activities include: Keynote speeches, panel discussions, skills workshops, networking sessions, and showcase of girls' projects.

Girls' Achievement Awards

An initiative that recognizes and celebrates exceptional achievements by teenage girls in academics, leadership, community service, arts, sports, and innovation.

Impact: 50 girls recognized annually, with awards including educational scholarships, technology packages, and mentorship opportunities.

Our Program Model

Our programs follow a systematic approach that ensures relevance, effectiveness, and sustainability. We begin with thorough needs assessment and community engagement, design evidence-based interventions, implement with strong support systems, rigorously monitor and evaluate, and develop strategies for scale and sustainability.

Throughout this process, we emphasize participatory approaches that involve girls, families, and communities in program design, implementation, and evaluation.
