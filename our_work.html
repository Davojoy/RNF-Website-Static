Our Work

Creating sustainable impact in the lives of teenage girls

Our Work

At RNF, we take a comprehensive approach to addressing the challenges facing teenage girls in marginalized communities. Our work spans multiple areas including education, health, empowerment, and community engagement, all aimed at creating lasting positive change.

Our Approach

We believe that sustainable development happens when we address both immediate needs and long-term structural barriers. Our programs are designed with these key principles in mind:

  • Holistic Support:

    Addressing multiple aspects of girls' development.

  • Community Involvement:

    Engaging families and communities as partners.

  • Girl-Centered Design:

    Involving girls in program design and decision-making.

  • Evidence-Based:

    Using research and data to inform our approaches.

  • Sustainable Impact:

    Building local capacity for long-term change.

Key Areas of Focus

Education

We work to ensure that teenage girls have access to quality education and the support they need to succeed academically.

  • Scholarships and school fee support
  • After-school tutoring programs
  • Digital literacy training
  • Career guidance and mentorship

Health & Wellbeing

We promote physical and mental health through education, awareness, and access to services.

  • Reproductive health education
  • Mental health support and counseling
  • Nutrition programs
  • Access to health services

Empowerment

We equip girls with the skills, confidence, and resources to become leaders and changemakers.

  • Leadership development programs
  • Life skills training
  • Economic empowerment initiatives
  • Peer mentorship networks

Community Engagement

We work with families, schools, and communities to create supportive environments for girls.

  • Parent education workshops
  • Teacher training programs
  • Community awareness campaigns
  • Male engagement initiatives

Where We Work

Our Reach

We currently operate in 25 communities across 5 counties, with plans to expand to additional regions in the coming years. Our primary focus areas include:

  • Urban Informal Settlements: Working with girls in densely populated areas with limited resources.
  • Rural Communities: Reaching girls in remote areas where access to education and services is challenging.
  • Refugee Settings: Supporting displaced girls who face unique vulnerabilities.

We select our program locations based on needs assessments, community interest, and potential for partnership with local stakeholders.
